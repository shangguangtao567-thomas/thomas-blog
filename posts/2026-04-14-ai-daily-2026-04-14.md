### AlphaEvolve Found 23 Breakthroughs in Pure Mathematics. The Mountain Is Still There.

2025 was the turning point. Multiple AI systems had already solved 5 out of 6 IMO problems, stunning the mathematical community. But the real shift came when AI moved from competition problems into genuine research mathematics — discovering new theorems, proving open conjectures, and verifying results at a pace that left mathematicians reassessing their craft.

DeepMind's AlphaEvolve, a system that uses Gemini to write and evolve programs via genetic algorithms, was run by a four-mathematician team — including Fields Medalist Terence Tao — on 67 open problems in combinatorics and algebra. It found improvements over the best known solutions on 23 of them, at a pace the authors described as "a day or two per problem" versus months for human experts. Among the most striking: AlphaEvolve discovered that Bruhat intervals in certain permutation groups form hypercubes when the deck size is a power of 2 — a structure mathematicians hadn't anticipated. The code it generated shrank from roughly 50 lines to about 5 on these special cases.

The February 2026 First Proof challenge gave entrants one week to have AI models work through 10 research-level questions chosen to avoid training contamination. More than half were solved. UCLA's Ernest Ryu went further: he used ChatGPT to prove Yurii Nesterov's 42-year-old convergence result for accelerated gradient descent in roughly 12 hours of human-AI collaboration — a paper he said "could be published in a top optimization journal without the AI component." He subsequently took a leave of absence to join OpenAI's technical staff.

Mathematicians are cautiously effusive. Daniel Litt wrote that "it's very likely that this technology is bigger than the computer." Terence Tao's own analogy: humans are strategic climbers planning routes up mountain ranges; current AIs are "jumping robots" that can "parkour their way to the top of a 6-foot wall" but cannot do long-term strategic climbing.

### OpenAI Acquires Hiro Finance — A Quiet Acqui-hire in Personal Financial Planning

OpenAI has acquired Hiro Finance, a personal financial AI startup, the company announced this week. Hiro will shut down operations on April 20 and delete all user data on May 13 — a timeline consistent with a classic acqui-hire structure, where the primary asset is the team rather than the product itself.

Hiro was founded in 2023 by Ethan Bloch, who previously built digital savings bank Digit, sold to Oportun in 2021 for approximately $230 million. Hiro's AI tool allowed users to input salary, debt, and monthly expenses; the system would then model different financial "what-if" scenarios to assist decision-making. The company specialized in accuracy training for financial calculations — a domain where precision matters more than general reasoning.

Bloch's team will join OpenAI intact. The acquisition signals OpenAI's continued interest in building domain-specific AI capabilities for financial planning, an area where the stakes of AI error are high and where regulatory requirements create barriers that general-purpose AI cannot easily clear.

### Stanford AI Index Report: 84% of Experts See AI Improving Healthcare. Only 44% of the Public Agrees

Stanford University's annual AI Index Report, released this week, documents a growing chasm between how AI researchers view the technology and how the general public does. The divergence is stark across every major application domain.

In healthcare, 84% of AI experts believe AI will have a positive impact; among the general public, that figure drops to 44%. On employment, 73% of experts are optimistic versus just 23% of the public. Sixty-four percent of Americans believe AI will cause significant job displacement within 20 years. Only 10% of Americans report feeling excited rather than concerned about AI in their daily lives.

On AI governance, the US ranks last among surveyed countries in trusting the government to regulate AI responsibly — just 31%, compared to 81% in Singapore. The report also notes that Generation Z is leading the increase in negative sentiment, even as roughly half of young adults report using AI tools daily.

The gap has practical consequences. As AI systems become more capable, public resistance creates political pressure for restrictions that researchers believe would hamper beneficial development. The report stops short of prescribing solutions, but its data makes clear that the technical and social trajectories of AI are increasingly misaligned.

### Honor Launches YOYO Claw: An On-Device AI Agent That Outperforms OpenClaw at Half the Token Cost

Honor launched YOYO Claw, its self-developed on-device AI agent, at an event on April 13. The system debuts on the MagicBook Pro 14 laptop, with 5 primary agents and 23 sub-agents covering education, productivity, and research scenarios.

Honor claims significant efficiency gains over OpenClaw: a 50% reduction in average token consumption through intelligent task routing that directs high-frequency tasks to the local device (zero token cost) and escalates complex tasks to cloud inference. The company's own lab benchmarks, measured on the PinchBench test set, show OpenClaw at 89.5% task success versus YOYO Claw at 94.5%.

Security is handled by a dedicated safety agent that monitors AI operations and blocks high-risk actions such as disk formatting. All user data remains on-device. The MagicBook Pro 14 delivers up to 16.7 hours of battery life.

The launch positions Honor as a direct competitor to OpenClaw in the personal AI agent market, with an approach that emphasizes local execution and cost efficiency over cloud-centric architectures.

### Cloudflare Agent Cloud Goes Live with GPT-5.4 and Codex: 15 Billion Tokens Per Minute Processed

Cloudflare announced the general availability of GPT-5.4 and Codex through its Agent Cloud platform, making OpenAI's frontier models accessible to millions of enterprise customers through Cloudflare's global network of over 100 data centers.

Enterprises can now deploy AI agents within Cloudflare's Workers AI environment — a production-safe sandbox — to automate customer support, update systems, and generate reports. The Codex harness is available in Cloudflare Sandbox today, with Workers AI support incoming. OpenAI's models were previously accessible only through direct API integration; Agent Cloud adds a managed deployment layer with Cloudflare's network-level security and latency optimization.

Key metrics: over one million enterprise customers now use OpenAI directly; Codex has 3 million weekly active users; the OpenAI API processes over 15 billion tokens per minute at peak load. The partnership with Cloudflare extends this reach into Cloudflare's enterprise customer base, bringing frontier AI capabilities to businesses that lack the infrastructure to deploy API-based AI systems directly.
