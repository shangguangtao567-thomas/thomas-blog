# AI Daily | 2026-04-14 | AI Crosses the Research-Level Math Threshold

The inflection point arrived this cycle: AI systems solved over half of research-level mathematics problems in 2026 — up from 5 out of 6 IMO problems just months earlier. Terence Tao, Fields Medalist at UCLA, put it plainly in a Quanta Magazine interview: "For the first time, it does feel like we could formalize a significant fraction of mathematics through AI." The hardware layer enabling longer reasoning chains also moved: China's first domestically produced HBM3 chip reached 819GB/s bandwidth, removing a memory-wall bottleneck that analysts say is now the rate-limiting factor for sustained AI proof search. And wide distribution via Cloudflare (3M weekly Codex users, 15B tokens/min processed) means the tools are no longer confined to AI labs.

## Lead Stories

### AlphaEvolve Found 23 Discoveries. The Mountain Is Still There.

Two developments converged this cycle to mark a genuine phase transition in AI-assisted mathematical research. The first is AlphaEvolve, DeepMind's system that uses Gemini to write and evolve programs via genetic algorithms. In a paper published late 2025, a four-mathematician team — including Fields Medalist Terence Tao — reported running AlphaEvolve on 67 open problems in combinatorics and algebra. The system found improvements over the best known solutions on 23 of them, at a pace the authors described as "a day or two per problem" versus months for human experts. Among the more striking results: AlphaEvolve discovered that Bruhat intervals in certain permutation groups form higher-dimensional cubes — hypercubes — when the deck size is a power of 2, a structure mathematicians hadn't anticipated. The code it generated shrank from roughly 50 lines to about 5 when operating on these special cases.

The second development is the February 2026 First Proof challenge, which gave entrants a week to have AI models work through 10 research-level questions chosen to avoid training contamination. More than half were solved with varying levels of human guidance. UCLA's Ernest Ryu went further: he used ChatGPT to prove a 42-year-old open problem — Yurii Nesterov's 1983 convergence result for accelerated gradient descent — in roughly 12 hours of human-AI collaboration. The resulting paper, he said, "could be published in a top optimization journal without the AI component." He has since taken a leave of absence from UCLA to join OpenAI's technical staff.

Mathematicians are cautiously effusive. Daniel Litt of the University of Toronto wrote that "it's very likely that this technology is bigger than the computer." But Fields Medalist Akshay Venkatesh urged caution: "There are valuable things in our culture which we should try to keep." Tao's own analogy is architectural: humans plan long-term routes up mountain ranges; current AIs are "jumping robots" that can "parkour their way to the top of a 6-foot wall" but can't do strategic climbing.

### China's First Domestic HBM3 Just Hit 819GB/s — And It Matters for AI Reasoning

When AI systems celebrated solving research-level math this cycle, they were cheering software. But the hardware enabling that leap is now visible: memory bandwidth.

Shenzhen-based Yuanjian Zhichu (远见智存) released HBM3/3e chips delivering 819GB/s bandwidth on a 1024-bit bus — roughly an order of magnitude above DDR5's 64-bit interface. The company, founded in 2023 by engineers who began working on HBM in 2016, holds full IP across DRAM die to base die, with a fully Chinese supply chain in design, wafer fabrication, and packaging. TSV redundancy layout improves chip yield by roughly 8%, reducing wafer cost per functional unit.

Analyst Ming-Chi Kuo put it plainly: memory bandwidth, not compute, is the bottleneck for extended reasoning. KV Cache compression cannot eliminate memory demand — it is a system-level challenge. As reasoning chains grow longer and proof searches run deeper, the memory wall becomes the rate-limiting factor. Yole Group projects the global HBM market will exceed $46 billion in 2026, growing to nearly $100 billion by 2030 at a 33% CAGR. With global HBM supply historically dominated by SK Hynix, Samsung, and Micron, a credible Chinese entrant at the 819GB/s tier is a meaningful supply-chain data point as AI labs push toward sustained, stateful reasoning at scale.

**Uncertainty-gated CoT halves the cost of generative reward modeling**
Researchers can now selectively activate chain-of-thought reasoning only when a model is uncertain — skipping expensive multi-step inference on easy queries. E-GRM (UNSW, Peking University, Tencent, ACL 2026) uses the model's own convergence behavior across parallel generations as a task-agnostic difficulty signal: if generations agree, CoT is bypassed; if they diverge, multi-step reasoning triggers. The result is substantial compute reduction without accuracy loss — making sustained proof search economically viable at scale.
