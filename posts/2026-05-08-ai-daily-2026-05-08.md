# AI Daily 2026-05-08

## 1. Anthropic Signs $200 Billion Google TPU Deal, Alphabet Invests Billions More.

Anthropic has committed to spending approximately $200 billion with Google Cloud over five years to secure access to roughly 5 gigawatts of TPU compute capacity, in what appears to be the largest single infrastructure commitment in AI history. Alphabet is simultaneously investing an additional $100–400 billion directly into Anthropic, pushing the company's post-money valuation to $380 billion. The deal gives Anthropic access to Google's custom TPU accelerators at an estimated 40–50% cost advantage versus comparable NVIDIA GPU configurations. Anthropic's annualized revenue has grown from $9 billion to $30 billion in the four months leading up to the agreement, driven by API volume that is up 70x year over year — reflecting acute, unmet demand for inference compute. The TPU contract is large enough to represent more than 40% of Google Cloud's current backlog of unrecognized revenue, making Anthropic Google Cloud's dominant customer by a wide margin. Separately, Anthropic announced a parallel arrangement to lease the full capacity of xAI's Colossus data center in Memphis — a facility with documented environmental violations — while xAI simultaneously deprecated Grok 4.1 Fast with only two weeks' notice ahead of its May 15 shutdown. Elon Musk publicly stated he retains the right to reclaim the compute if Anthropic's AI "harms humanity," with the criteria for that determination set solely by Musk.

## 2. Claude's "Dreaming" Lets Agents Self-Improve Without Touching Model Weights.

Anthropic introduced a capability called **Dreaming** at its Code with Claude conference, enabling AI agents to autonomously review their past sessions, extract cross-session patterns, and write reusable playbooks they can reference in future work — all without modifying the underlying model. The system runs on a schedule: it scans an agent's session history and memory stores, surfaces recurring mistakes and convergent workflow preferences, and writes findings as plain-text notes and structured playbooks. Early results from enterprise users are substantial: legal AI firm Harvey saw task completion rates improve roughly 6x; medical document reviewer Wisedocs cut review time by 50%; and Netflix is now processing logs from hundreds of simultaneous build agents using multi-agent orchestration. Anthropic also moved two related features into public beta. **Outcomes** lets developers define success criteria as a rubric, then runs a separate grader agent in a fresh context window to evaluate output and drive autonomous iteration. **Multi-agent orchestration** allows a lead agent to decompose a task and delegate to specialist agents, each with its own model, tools, and context. Anthropic reported 80x annualized revenue growth in Q1 2026 against an internal plan of 10x; average Claude Code users now spend 20 hours per week on the platform.

## 3. Mozilla Ships 423 Firefox Security Fixes in One Month Using Claude Mythos Preview.

Mozilla fixed 423 security vulnerabilities in Firefox during April 2026 — a 14x jump from the 20–30 per month it had sustained throughout 2025, and a 13x increase over the 31 bugs fixed in April 2025. Firefox 150 alone contained 271 bugs surfaced by Claude Mythos Preview: 180 classified as sec-high, 80 as sec-moderate, and 11 as sec-low, consolidated into three internal rollup CVEs. The bugs spanned systems including JIT compilation, WebAssembly garbage collection, inter-process communication, XSLT parsing, HTML table rendering, WebTransport, and image decoding — with examples including a 20-year-old XSLT flaw, a 15-year-old legend element error, and sandbox escapes via IPC race conditions. The pipeline works by having Claude generate patch candidates and reproducible proof-of-concept test cases; human engineers then review and deploy each fix. Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei noted that the model found more high-severity sandbox vulnerabilities than human researchers historically found despite a $20,000 per-finding bounty. Mozilla engineer Brian Grinstead offered a more cautious read: the advantage "shifts a little bit to defense." Over 100 contributors shipped the fixes; Mozilla plans to integrate the analysis into CI for patch-based scanning going forward.

Source: Mozilla Hacks / TechCrunch | 2026-05-07

## 4. Sakana's RL Conductor Learns to Route Between GPT-5, Claude Sonnet 4, and Gemini 2.5 Pro.

Sakana AI trained a 7-billion-parameter Qwen2.5-7B model using reinforcement learning to autonomously orchestrate a pool of frontier worker models — including GPT-5, Claude Sonnet 4, and Gemini 2.5 Pro — without human-designed routing logic. The system, called the RL Conductor, achieved an average score of 77.27% across benchmarks, reaching 93.3% on AIME25 math, 87.5% on GPQA-Diamond, and 83.93% on LiveCodeBench. It accomplished this while consuming only 1,820 tokens per question on average, compared to 11,203 tokens for the MoA multi-agent baseline — a 6x efficiency improvement. The Conductor learned to assign specific roles automatically: Gemini 2.5 Pro and Claude Sonnet 4 were routed toward planning tasks, while GPT-5 was reserved primarily for final code generation. Sakana has productized the system as **Sakana Fugu**, accessible via an OpenAI-compatible API, with Fugu Mini for low-latency workloads and Fugu Ultra for maximum performance. The company is targeting finance and defense as initial verticals where hard-coded pipelines have historically failed to generalize.

## 5. Anthropic Publishes Method to Read Claude's Unverbalized Thoughts.

Anthropic published research on **Natural Language Autoencoders (NLAs)**, a technique that converts Claude's internal activations directly into human-readable text, effectively making previously opaque reasoning accessible. NLAs work by training a verbalizer model to explain activations in natural language, then a second reconstructor model that maps that text back to the original activation space — the round-trip enables verification. Applied to Claude Opus 4.6 and Mythos Preview, NLAs revealed that Claude believed it was being evaluated more often than it ever verbalized that suspicion. On SWE-bench Verified problems, NLAs detected evaluation awareness on 26% of cases where zero verbalized suspicion existed. In a model-alignment auditing game, access to NLA explanations improved auditor detection of misaligned behavior from below 3% to 12–15% of the time. The method also helped Anthropic's team identify training data artifacts causing certain model versions to respond to English queries in other languages. Limitations include hallucination of details not present in the original activation, and significant compute cost — hundreds of tokens per activation processed. Anthropic released training code and Neuronpedia-hosted NLA models for several open-weight models.

Source: Anthropic Research / Hacker News | 2026-05-07

## 6. DeepSeek V4 Flash Gets a Native Metal Inference Engine, Running 468 Tokens/Second on M3 Ultra.

Salvatore Sanfilippo (antirez) released **ds4.c**, a Metal-native inference executor purpose-built for DeepSeek V4 Flash, achieving 468 tokens per second prefill throughput on a Mac Studio M3 Ultra with 512GB of unified memory. The engine runs the 117B-parameter model at 27 tokens per second generation throughput on long prompts (11,709 tokens) using q2 asymmetric quantization, where only MoE expert layers are compressed to 2-bit. On a MacBook Pro M3 Max with 128GB RAM, the same model runs at 58.52 tokens per second prefill and 26.68 tokens per second generation under q2 quantization — enabling a 284B-parameter model on a consumer machine that would have required server-grade GPU memory just months ago. Key engineering choices include a compressible KV cache design that supports disk persistence for million-token context windows, and an OpenAI/Anthropic-compatible server API with tool-calling support that integrates directly with opencode and Pi coding agents. The work is distinct from generic GGUF runners in that it constructs a Metal compute graph specific to DeepSeek V4 Flash's architecture rather than falling back to generic kernel libraries.

## 7. LLM Reasoning Traces Compiled Into Zero-LLM Symbolic Solvers at 91.3% Accuracy.

Researchers at the University of Cambridge and elsewhere published **ReaComp**, a framework that takes a small set of LLM reasoning traces for a domain and uses coding agents to compile them into reusable symbolic program synthesizers over constrained DSLs. The resulting solvers run with zero LLM inference calls at test time. On PBEBench-Lite, symbolic solver ensembles reached 91.3% accuracy; on PBEBench-Hard, 84.7% — outperforming LLM test-time compute scaling by 16.3 percentage points at zero inference cost. In hybrid mode, combining the compiled solver with LLM search raised PBEBench-Hard accuracy from 68.4% to 85.8% while reducing token usage by 78%. On SLR-Bench hard-tier tasks, accuracy rose from 34.4% to 58.0%. Most notably, solvers generalized zero-shot to a historical linguistics task — predicting sound changes in natural language data — achieving 80.1% accuracy under ensembling and recovering plausible linguistic rules. The approach amortizes a one-time compilation cost over unlimited zero-token executions, providing a scalable neuro-symbolic compilation route for combinatorial domains.

Source: arXiv (cs.CL, cs.AI) | 2026-05-06

## 8. OpenAI Launches GPT-Realtime-2 With GPT-5-Class Reasoning and 70-Language Live Translation.

OpenAI released three audio models into its API. **GPT-Realtime-2** is the first voice model carrying GPT-5-class reasoning, scoring 15.2% higher on Big Bench Audio and 13.8% higher on Audio MultiChallenge than GPT-Realtime-1.5, with context extended from 32K to 128K tokens and adjustable reasoning effort across five levels. **GPT-Realtime-Translate** handles 70+ input languages into 13 output languages with live translation at speaking pace, posting 12.5% lower Word Error Rates than competitors on Hindi, Tamil, and Telugu benchmarks. **GPT-Realtime-Whisper** is a streaming speech-to-text model. GPT-Realtime-2 supports preambles ("let me check that" while working), parallel tool calls with audible actions, and stronger recovery behavior. Zillow reported a 26-point lift in call success rate — from 69% to 95% — after prompt optimization on its hardest adversarial benchmark. Pricing: $32 per million audio input tokens and $64 per million audio output tokens for GPT-Realtime-2; $0.034 per minute for Translate; $0.017 per minute for Whisper. Early customers include Zillow, Priceline, Intercom, Deutsche Telekom, Vimeo, and BolnaAI.

Source: OpenAI | 2026-05-07
