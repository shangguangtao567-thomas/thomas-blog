# AI Daily · May 10, 2026

## 1. OpenAI Governance Trial: Brockman Testifies Musk Demanded Majority Equity and Board Control as OpenAI Weighs $1T IPO.

In the second week of the landmark Musk v. Altman trial, OpenAI president Greg Brockman delivered detailed testimony contradicting Musk's narrative, telling the jury that Musk himself pushed OpenAI to create a for-profit entity in 2017 and demanded majority equity, the right to appoint a majority of board members, and the CEO role. Brockman recounted that Musk abruptly walked out of a negotiation meeting after being offered equal equity stakes among cofounders, grabbing a Tesla-themed painting in anger. Shivon Zilis, a former OpenAI board member and mother of several of Musk's children, testified separately that Musk attempted to recruit Sam Altman to lead a prospective AI lab at Tesla in 2017. The trial could disrupt OpenAI's path to an IPO approaching $1 trillion in valuation. Meanwhile, xAI and SpaceX — now operating as a combined entity — are targeting a public listing as early as June 2026 at a valuation of $1.75 trillion. Brockman holds a stake estimated at $30 billion in the company.

## 2. Gemini API File Search Now Supports Multimodal RAG with Native Image Retrieval and Page-Level Citations.

Google DeepMind announced three updates to the Gemini API File Search tool: native multimodal indexing, custom metadata filters, and page-level citations. File Search now embeds images and text together using Gemini Embedding 2, allowing applications to search visual archives using natural-language descriptions of tone, style, or content without relying on filenames or alt text alone. Developers can attach key-value metadata labels such as `department: Legal` or `status: Final` and filter retrieval at query time, reducing noise and improving speed in RAG pipelines. Page citations now capture exact PDF page numbers, letting applications point answers back to the source page for verification. Google is positioning the feature as managed, verifiable RAG infrastructure for production AI applications.

Source: Google DeepMind Blog (via Hacker News) | 2026-05-05

## 3. DELEGATE-52 Benchmark Finds Frontier LLMs Corrupt an Average of 25% of Document Content in Long Delegated Workflows.

A new arXiv paper introduces DELEGATE-52, a benchmark spanning 52 professional domains — including coding, crystallography, and music notation — that evaluates how reliably frontier LLMs execute multi-step document-editing workflows over extended interactions. Researchers tested 19 LLMs and found that even the strongest named models — Gemini 3.1 Pro, Claude 4.6 Opus, and GPT 5.4 — corrupt approximately 25% of document content by the end of long delegated workflows. Additional experiments found that agentic tool use does not improve DELEGATE-52 performance, and degradation worsens with larger documents, longer interaction histories, and the presence of distractor files. The paper concludes that current frontier models are unreliable delegates because sparse but severe errors silently compound over extended interactions.

Source: arXiv / Philippe Laban et al. (via Hacker News) | 2026-04-17

## 4. DigitalOcean Launches Five-Layer AI-Native Cloud Stack Built Around Open-Source Primitives and MCP.

DigitalOcean unveiled its AI-Native Cloud at Deploy 2026, a five-layer platform spanning Managed Agents, Data & Learning, Inference Engine, Core Cloud, and Infrastructure. The stack runs open-source components including PostgreSQL, MySQL, MongoDB, Valkey, OpenSearch, Kafka, Weaviate, vLLM, SGLang, LangGraph, and CrewAI, with GPU options covering NVIDIA HGX B300 and AMD Instinct MI350X alongside H100 and H200. The Inference Router resolves user intent in 200 milliseconds and routes requests by cost, latency, and quality. A customer case study from Celiums.AI reports that routing 83% of 29.2 million tokens to open-source models via the Router reduced per-token cost by 61% while keeping p95 latency under 400 milliseconds. Knowledge Bases are exposed as MCP tools by default. Managed Agents include Durable State Management, Managed Sandboxes, and an MCP client.

Source: DigitalOcean Blog (via Hacker News) | 2026-05-05

Chinese AI lab DeepSeek is reportedly pursuing a fundraising round of up to 50 billion RMB (approximately $7 billion), targeting a post-money valuation of roughly $45 billion, according to local media. Reports describe DeepSeek V4 as a trillion-parameter Mixture of Experts model trained end-to-end on Huawei's Ascend hardware — a notable claim given U.S. export restrictions on advanced AI chips to China. The funding round is framed as a vehicle for employee equity repricing, control arrangements, and deeper integration with China's domestic compute ecosystem. The report notes that DeepSeek's team remains lean at under 200 people, and that core researcher retention has made equity incentives urgent. The round would rank among the largest AI financings in China to date.

## 6. Baidu DuMate Tops Agent Benchmarks, Outperforming Anthropic and OpenAI Models on PinchBench.

Baidu's DuMate agent framework achieved first and second place on PinchBench with scores of 93.3% and 93.2%, outperforming Anthropic's Claude (89.0%) and OpenAI's model (91.6%) in the same scenarios. PinchBench covers 23 real-world work scenarios and 147 tasks, measuring success rate, speed, and cost. DuMate also ranked first on the DeepResearch Bench with a composite score of 58.03. Baidu attributes the results to a "harness" architecture — an execution stack that lifts the underlying model's capabilities toward its theoretical ceiling in real task environments rather than relying on benchmark-specific tuning. The benchmark coverage spans both single-turn and long-horizon agent tasks.

Source: 雷峰网 (via 36氪 AI Daily) | 2026-05-09

## 7. Wispr Flow India Growth Doubles After Local Push, Highlighting Voice AI's Multilingual Consumer Challenge.

Wispr Flow, the AI-powered voice input startup, reported that India growth accelerated from roughly 60% month-over-month to around 100% following a dedicated local push including Hinglish voice model beta testing and an Android launch. India has become Wispr Flow's second-largest market by users and revenue after the United States. The startup's India country manager, Nimisha Mehta, is leading an expansion to roughly 30 India employees over the next year. Despite 2.5 million global downloads between October 2025 and April 2026 — with India accounting for 14% of installs — India contributes only about 2% of in-app purchase revenue, reflecting the monetization gap in the market. Wispr Flow plans to eventually price subscriptions as low as ₹10–20 per month (roughly $0.10–0.20) to reach beyond urban, white-collar users into mass-market households. India is described by analysts as "the ultimate stress test for voice AI" given its linguistic, accent, and contextual diversity.

Source: TechCrunch | 2026-05-09
