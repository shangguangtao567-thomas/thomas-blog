# AI Daily 2026-05-09

## 1. Anthropic Signs Compute Deal Using All of xAI's Colossus Data Center, Raising Ethical and Supply-Chain Questions.

Anthropic has signed an agreement to use the full capacity of SpaceX and xAI's Colossus 1 data center in Memphis, one of the world's largest AI training clusters. The deal gives Anthropic access to more than 300 megawatts of compute power. The Colossus facility has attracted criticism for its environmental footprint, with local regulators scrutinizing its water and electricity consumption. Elon Musk, who controls xAI and SpaceX, has stated publicly: "We reserve the right to reclaim compute if their AI engages in actions that harm humanity." Separately, xAI deprecated its Grok 4.1 Fast model with just two weeks of notice, raising questions about the stability of commercial AI commitments. Meanwhile, Anthropic is also party to a separate, massive commitment: the company has pledged approximately $200 billion to Google Cloud over five years for TPU access — the largest chip rental agreement in history — giving it a combined compute posture that far exceeds any other AI safety-focused lab. Anthropic's API volume grew 17x year-on-year in the period leading up to these deals, with rate limits doubled for Claude Code across Pro, Max, and Enterprise tiers.

An IPO is rumored for October 2026 at a valuation exceeding $900 billion. ## 3. Mozilla Found 271 Firefox Security Bugs with Claude Mythos Preview in a Single Month, Jumping from 20-30 per Month to 423.

Mozilla's security team resolved 423 security bugs in Firefox version 150 during April 2026, compared to a typical monthly rate of 20 to 30 in 2025. Of those, 271 bugs were attributed to Claude Mythos Preview — the company's internal AI agent — across a three-week scanning period. The breakdown was 180 rated sec-high, 80 sec-moderate, and 11 sec-low. Notable discoveries included a 20-year-old XSLT vulnerability and a 15-year-old `<legend>` element flaw. Mozilla had built an agentic testing harness using Claude Opus 4.6, running across ephemeral virtual machines at scale, with the pipeline now integrated into the project's continuous integration system. The result was a 14x improvement over the normal bug discovery rate, achieved without an expansion of the security team. Three CVE rollups were issued to cover the bulk of the findings: CVE-2026-6784 (154 bugs), CVE-2026-6785 (55 bugs), and CVE-2026-6786 (107 bugs).

## 4. Cloudflare Cuts 1,100 Jobs — 20% of Its Workforce — While Posting Record Revenue, Citing AI Efficiency Gains.

Cloudflare cut 1,100 positions in May 2026, representing 20% of its total workforce, even as the company posted record quarterly revenue of $639.8 million — a 34% year-on-year increase. CEO Matthew Prince directly attributed the layoffs to AI: internal AI usage across engineering, HR, finance, and marketing teams had grown more than 600% in three months, with employees running thousands of AI agent sessions daily. Prince stated that "support people that provide support behind them — those roles aren't going to drive companies forward," signaling that roles automating internal workflows were the primary target. The company simultaneously operates one of the largest AI agent security review pipelines in the industry, having reported that 100% of vibe-coded deployments at Cloudflare are reviewed by autonomous AI agents. The combination of record revenue and major workforce reduction sets a template other tech companies are expected to follow.

## 7. Google's AlphaEvolve AI Designs Next-Generation TPU Circuits That Were Directly Integrated Into Silicon.

Google DeepMind published a one-year report on AlphaEvolve, its evolutionary AI system for hardware and mathematical discovery. The system proposed what engineers described as "counter-intuitive" circuit layouts for a next-generation Tensor Processing Unit, and those designs were directly fabricated and integrated into actual silicon — marking the first time an AI-designed chip went from layout proposal to physical production within Google's own stack. The system was also used in collaboration with mathematician Terence Tao to discover solutions to open problems in Erdős-type combinatorics, generating quantum circuit designs that reduced error rates by a factor of ten in targeted domains. Broader deployments include a 30% reduction in DNA sequencing error rates at partner institutions, a doubling of training throughput at Klarna, and a fourfold acceleration in drug screening pipelines. Jeff Dean summarized the recursive dynamic: "the TPU brain is designing the next TPU body." Separately, an Anthropic cofounder estimated a 60% probability of a fully autonomous AI system that can independently train its own successor emerging by the end of 2028.

## 9. Former OpenAI CTO Mira Murati Testifies That Sam Altman Misled Her on Safety Reviews, Exposing Governance Crisis.

Mira Murati, who served as OpenAI's CTO until mid-2025, testified under oath in Elon Musk's lawsuit against OpenAI that CEO Sam Altman repeatedly misled her regarding the safety review process for frontier models. Murati stated that Altman selectively provided different information to different executives, and that safety approval milestones were deliberately obscured rather than genuinely evaluated. She further testified that OpenAI's non-profit governance structure had been functionally emptied: talent, models, and valuable assets had all been transferred into the for-profit subsidiary, leaving the non-profit with nominal oversight but no operational control. The core of Musk's lawsuit alleges that Altman violated OpenAI's founding charitable mission — to develop AI openly for the benefit of all humanity, without profit-maximizing structures — and that the for-profit restructuring and Microsoft investment constituted a breach of that founding agreement. OpenAI has denied the allegations.

## 10. Airbnb Discloses That AI Now Writes 60% of Its New Code and an AI Bot Handles 40% of Customer Issues Without Human Escalation.
