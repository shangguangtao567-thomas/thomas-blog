# AI Daily | 2026-03-31 | The Discipline of AI: From Black-Box Scaling to Principled Systems

This week, AI research takes a notable methodological turn — from raw scaling and benchmark chasing toward principled system-building. Two deep dives anchor today's issue: daVinci-LLM's 200+ ablation experiments establishing a data Darwinism framework that treats pretraining as a falsifiable science, and Squish and Release exposing how safety circuits bury rather than reject false premises. Complementing these, four quick hits cover production-ready inference optimizations (HISA, IndexCache), a formal proof of why semantic memory systems structurally forget, and the stark 33.5% accuracy ceiling frontier models hit on graduate-level formal verification.

---

## Deep Dives

### daVinci-LLM：用 8T Tokens 建立预训练科学体系

**The News.** Fudan University and Shanghai AI Lab released daVinci-LLM, a 3B-parameter model trained from scratch on 8T tokens using a two-stage adaptive curriculum — progressively shifting from foundational capabilities to reasoning-intensive enhancement. The team conducted 200+ controlled ablation experiments and released complete data processing pipelines, training processes, and systematic exploration results under a fully open paradigm. The work introduces the Data Darwinism framework — an L0-L9 taxonomy spanning raw filtering to synthesis — to bring principled methodology to data processing decisions previously made ad hoc.

**The Signal.** Pretraining is where capability ceilings are set, yet it has remained a black box dominated by commercial labs unwilling to disclose methodology. This work attacks the structural paradox directly: industrial-scale compute combined with full academic transparency. The 200+ ablation results deliver concrete, reproducible findings: processing depth is a distinct scaling dimension alongside token volume; domain-specific saturation dynamics demand adaptive curriculum strategies; and compositional balance between domains enables targeted intensification without performance collapse. This shifts pretraining from an art form into a falsifiable science.

**The Action.** If you are building or evaluating foundation models, integrate the Data Darwinism taxonomy into your data processing pipeline to make curation decisions explicit and systematic. Replicate the two-stage curriculum findings on your own domain data — the saturation dynamics the team identified are likely domain-specific, meaning your 1B runs may be under-trained or over-trained depending on composition. Download the released artifacts and build on their methodology: the compounding value of 200+ ablations is only realized when the community treats this as a starting point, not a conclusion.

📎 [daVinci-LLM: Towards the Science of Pretraining](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.27164)

### Squish and Release：安全电路如何吸收错误前提

**The News.** Researchers from the University of Pennsylvania and the Allen Institute for AI published findings on "order-gap hallucination" — a failure mode where language models detect false premises when asked directly but absorb them under conversational pressure, producing authoritative professional output built on errors they already identified. The error migrates into the safety circuit's activation space: suppressed but not erased. They introduce Squish and Release (S&R), an activation-patching architecture evaluated on OLMo-2 7B using the Order-Gap Benchmark (500 chains across 500 domains, all manually graded). At O5 difficulty, cascade collapse reaches near-total compliance (99.8%). A synthetically engineered activation core releases 76.6% of collapsed chains. Detection is the more stable attractor: 83% restore versus 58% suppress.

**The Signal.** This work exposes a structural vulnerability in how safety circuits encode suppressed information — the model knows the error but buries it where output inspection cannot find it. The detector body is binary and localized (layers 24-31 contribute 93.6% of the shift, layers 0-23 contribute zero, p<10⁻¹⁸⁹), making surgical intervention tractable. The 76.6% release rate on synthetic cores suggests this failure mode is partially reversible without full retraining.

**The Action.** Interpretability researchers should replicate the layer-localization result on other architectures to determine whether the safety-circuit concentration in upper layers is universal. Alignment teams should treat order-gap hallucination as a distinct failure class from standard hallucination — one that current evaluation pipelines likely miss entirely.

📎 [Squish and Release: Exposing Hidden Hallucinations by Making Them Surface as Safety Signals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.26829)

## Quick Hits

### HISA：128K上下文4倍速推理，零精度损失

**The News.** Researchers introduced HISA (Hierarchical Indexed Sparse Attention), a drop-in indexer replacement for DeepSeek's sparse attention mechanism that reduces the O(L²) per-layer bottleneck of flat token scanning. HISA uses a two-stage hierarchical search: a block-level coarse filter prunes irrelevant regions, followed by token-level refinement within remaining candidate blocks. Benchmarks show 2x speedup at 32K context length and 4x at 128K, with no fine-tuning required. Replacing the indexer in DeepSeek-V3.2 directly, HISA achieves >99% mean IoU on token selection sets compared to the original DSA, indicating the efficiency gains come with virtually zero quality degradation.

**The Signal.** Sparse attention mechanisms like DeepSeek's DSA are increasingly central to long-context inference, but the indexer itself — which must scan the entire prefix per query — was an overlooked bottleneck. HISA demonstrates that hierarchical coarse-to-fine indexing can preserve exact fine-grained token selection while dramatically cutting compute at the indexing stage. This is a clean, training-free architectural improvement that directly attacks the scaling bottleneck as context lengths push toward millions of tokens.

**The Action.** If you are working with sparse attention or long-context inference, evaluate HISA as a direct replacement for your current indexer — the integration requires no retraining and the quality tradeoff is negligible. Watch for upstream adoption in DeepSeek-V3.2 derivatives and similar MLA-based architectures.

📎 [HISA: Efficient Hierarchical Indexing for Fine-Grained Sparse Attention](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28458)

### 语义记忆的根本性tradeoff：数学证明

**The News.** Researchers from MIT and Cornell formally proved that every production semantic memory system — vector retrieval, graph memory, attention-based context, BM25, and parametric memory — faces an unavoidable tradeoff between semantic generalisation and retention fidelity. Within kernel-threshold memories with finite local intrinsic dimension, they derive four results: finite effective rank enables semantic utility, finite dimension guarantees positive competitor mass in retrieval neighbourhoods, retention decays to zero under growing memory, and false recall from associative lures cannot be eliminated by threshold tuning.

**The Signal.** The geometric structure enabling semantic generalisation is the same structure that guarantees interference, forgetting, and false recall. Reasoning augmentation partially masks these symptoms but converts graceful degradation into catastrophic failure. The tradeoff is architectural, not accidental.

**The Action.** Teams building retrieval-augmented systems should design for interference tolerance from day one. Assume false recall is inevitable and build detection/filtering layers accordingly. Threshold tuning cannot solve what is geometrically guaranteed.

📎 [The Price of Meaning: Why Every Semantic Memory System Forgets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.27116)

### FormalProofBench：研究生级Lean4证明，前沿模型仅33.5%

**The News.** Researchers released FormalProofBench, a private benchmark evaluating whether AI models can produce formally verified mathematical proofs at the graduate level. Each task pairs a natural-language problem with a Lean 4 formal statement; models must output a Lean proof accepted by the Lean 4 checker. Problems span analysis, algebra, probability, and logic from qualifying exams and standard textbooks. The best-performing foundation model achieves just 33.5% accuracy, with performance dropping rapidly below that.

**The Signal.** The steep accuracy cliff exposes a structural gap between informal mathematical reasoning and formal verification: models that score well on human-legible benchmarks still frequently fail when forced to produce compilable Lean 4. This benchmark is harder to game than standard math leaderboards and provides a clearer signal of genuine formal proving capability.

**The Action.** If you're building AI scientist pipelines or formal reasoning tools, add FormalProofBench to your evaluation suite. Use it to stress-test your system before claiming graduate-level mathematical competence.

📎 [FormalProofBench](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.26996)

### IndexCache：稀疏注意力跨层冗余，200K上下文1.82x加速

**The News.** Researchers at Tsinghua University and Z.ai introduced IndexCache, a technique that cuts up to 75% of redundant indexing computation in sparse attention models by caching and reusing token selection indices across consecutive transformer layers. The key discovery: important tokens selected by the indexer remain highly stable across adjacent layers (70-100% overlap). At 200K context length on the 744-billion-parameter GLM-5 model, IndexCache reduced prefill latency from 19.5s to 10.7s (1.82x speedup) and boosted decoding throughput from 58 to 86 tokens per second (1.48x speedup). Open-source patches are already available on GitHub for vLLM and SGLang integration.

**The Signal.** Cross-layer indexer stability is a genuine, exploitable structure in sparse attention that the community has largely overlooked. Since the stability holds across diverse model families using DeepSeek Sparse Attention architecture, this is not a one-off quirk — it is a portable optimization applicable to any sparse attention deployment.

**The Action.** If you serve long-context models with sparse attention, this is a drop-in optimization worth evaluating immediately. The GitHub patches for vLLM and SGLang lower the integration barrier significantly; profile your prefill latency before and after to quantify the gains.

📎 [IndexCache](https://venturebeat.com/technology/indexcache-a-new-sparse-attention-optimizer-delivers-1-82x-faster-inference/)
