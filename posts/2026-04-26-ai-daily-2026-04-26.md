# AI Daily 2026-04-26

## 1. DeepSeek V4 Open-Sources a Frontier-Competitive Model at 1/10th the Cost, with 1M-Token Context and Day-0 SGLang Support.

DeepSeek released V4-Pro and V4-Flash on April 24, immediately matching Claude-Opus-4.6 and GPT-5.4 on self-reported benchmarks while costing roughly one-tenth as much. V4-Pro has 1.6T total parameters (49B active via MoE) and V4-Flash has 284B total (13B active), both supporting a 1M-token context window under an MIT license. V4-Flash is priced at $0.14/M input tokens and $0.28/M output tokens — cheaper than GPT-5.4 Nano — while V4-Pro costs $1.74/M input and $3.48/M output, the cheapest large frontier model available. A new selective attention mechanism lets V4-Pro use only 27% of the compute and 10% of the KV cache compared to V3.2 at 1M-token context. The release is notable for also being the first DeepSeek model optimized for Huawei Ascend 950 Chinese chips, reducing dependence on Nvidia. SGLang and Miles shipped day-0 inference and RL training support with hybrid sparse-attention kernels (ShadowRadix prefix cache, HiSparse CPU-extended KV, FlashMLA, DeepGEMM Mega MoE), achieving under 10% throughput degradation from 4K to 900K tokens on B200 and H200 hardware. Miles also demonstrated RL training on 32 GB300 GPUs with FP8 rollout and BF16 training, with reward and eval scores growing steadily across the run.

## 2. The AI Compute Crunch Is Escalating: GitHub Copilot Pauses Signups, GPU Prices Hit 3.6×, Virginia Power Rates Projected +25%.

Multiple manifestations of an AI infrastructure strain surfaced this week. GitHub paused new Copilot signups and restricted access to more expensive AI models. Anthropic tested removing Claude Code from its $20/month plan, keeping it on the $100/month tier. OpenAI CFO Sarah Friar cited insufficient compute as the reason for shutting down Sora. Meta laid off 10% of its workforce on April 23, telling remaining staff the savings would offset data center and AI infrastructure investments. Consumer electronics pricing has been affected: the same 2TB external SSD that cost $159 in late 2025 now costs $575 — a 3.6× increase — as chip manufacturers shift capacity toward AI. Apple reportedly cannot secure enough chipmaking capacity for upcoming iPhones. Home electricity bills are rising sharply in states with high data center concentration. Nearly half of US data centers planned for 2026 have been canceled or delayed. The longer-term path to sustainability requires a renewable energy revolution, vastly increased chip manufacturing, more efficient models, and widely proven AI utility — all while AI continues to worsen energy consumption in the near term.

## 3. The AI Industry Is Facing Unprecedented Public Backlash, Including Violence Against OpenAI's CEO.

A populist backlash against AI is intensifying alongside the compute crunch. On April 10, OpenAI CEO Sam Altman's house was attacked with a Molotov cocktail by a 20-year-old suspect who cited AI existential risk concerns. Three days earlier in Indianapolis, an unknown perpetrator fired 13 shots into a local councilman's home over his support for a data center project. Stanford's annual AI Index (April 13, 2026) shows a stark expert-public gulf: 73% of AI experts view AI's long-term job impact positively versus 23% of the public, and 69% versus 21% on the economy. Gallup (March 2026) found Gen Z excitement about AI dropped from 36% to 22% while anger rose from 22% to 31%. A February 2026 NBER paper found 80% of companies actively using AI reported no impact on company productivity, and MIT 2025 data showed 95% of corporate AI pilot programs received zero return. Virginia — the epicenter of the US data center boom — projects residential electrical rates to increase up to 25% by 2030 due to AI infrastructure. A Futurism poll found AI companies less popular than ICE or Donald Trump. OpenAI has responded with an Industrial Policy White Paper proposing a Public Wealth Fund, while Microsoft released a Community-First AI Infrastructure Initiative to subsidize utility rates. OpenAI president Greg Brockman has funded a SuperPAC opposing state-level AI regulation, and OpenAI is lobbying for Illinois Senate Bill 3444 to shield the company from large-scale harms caused by AI models.

## 5. GPT-5.5 Unifies with Codex, Requires Fresh Prompt Tuning, and Launches a $25K Biosecurity Bug Bounty.

OpenAI confirmed that since GPT-5.4, Codex and the main model have been unified into a single system, and GPT-5.5 extends this unification with significant gains in agentic coding and computer use capabilities. OpenAI will not release a separate GPT-5.5-Codex model. GPT-5.5 is explicitly a new model family: the company advises against carrying over prompts from GPT-5.2 or 5.4, recommending fresh prompt tuning rather than migration. The migration path is $openai-docs migrate this project to gpt-5.5 for automatic upgrades. A new verbosity parameter (-o verbosity low/medium/high) is available. Separately, OpenAI launched a Bio Bug Bounty program for GPT-5.5 via Codex Desktop: participants who find one universal jailbreaking prompt that defeats five bio safety questions — without triggering moderation — can earn $25,000. Applications opened April 23; testing begins April 28 and runs through July 27, 2026.

Cohere has acquired Aleph Alpha in a transaction valued at approximately $20B, with German retail conglomerate Schwarz Group providing 500M euros (~$600M) in financing. ## 8. Verbal Process Supervision Introduces a Fourth Inference-Time Scaling Axis, Boosting GPT-5.4 to 94.9% on GPQA Diamond.

A new paper introduces Verbal Process Supervision (VPS), which adds a fourth axis of inference-time compute scaling: the granularity of external verbal supervision during reasoning. On GPQA Diamond, GPT-5.4 with VPS (R=4) reached 94.9%, surpassing the prior 94.1% SOTA without any gradient updates. On AIME 2025, weak-to-strong rescue boosted performance from 11.7–26.7% to 63.3–90.0% — a 63.3-point improvement. VPS outperformed Reflexion by +8.5 to +12.1 percentage points and Self-Consistency@5 by +5.0pp on GPQA and +8.3pp on LiveCodeBench. The approach scales with the supervisor-actor capability gap (Pearson r=0.90), meaning the benefit of VPS increases as the supervisor model is stronger relative to the actor. This formalizes critique granularity as an independent scaling lever alongside chain-of-thought length, search breadth, and candidate generation — a structurally novel contribution to the inference scaling literature.

## 9. Meituan Releases LongCat-2.0-Preview, a Trillion-Parameter Model with 1M-Token Context Optimized for Agent Workflows.

Meituan released LongCat-2.0-Preview on April 24, a trillion-parameter base model trained entirely on Chinese domestic compute infrastructure. The model supports a 1M-token context window, enabling single inferences that process millions of characters of input. LongCat-2.0 is deeply optimized for agent application scenarios including code generation, complex task planning, and enterprise automation workflows. The release marks Meituan's first trillion-parameter foundational model and demonstrates that Chinese domestic silicon can support training at the frontier scale — a significant data point in the ongoing compute sovereignty discussion. The model is currently in preview testing.
