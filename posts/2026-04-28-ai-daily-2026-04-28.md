# AI Daily 2026-04-28

## 1. OpenAI and Microsoft Dissolve Their AGI Revenue-Sharing Deal

OpenAI and Microsoft have ended their exclusive partnership agreement effective April 27, 2026, marking the collapse of one of the most consequential financial arrangements in AI history. Under the renegotiated terms, OpenAI can now serve customers across any cloud provider, not just Microsoft Azure. Microsoft's IP license remains in place through 2032 but is now non-exclusive, and Microsoft will no longer pay revenue share to OpenAI. OpenAI's payments to Microsoft continue through 2030, but are capped at a fixed total. The phrase "independent of OpenAI's technology progress" in the revised agreement signals that the AGI trigger clause is effectively dead — the original clause would have nullified Microsoft's IP rights if OpenAI achieved artificial general intelligence. Historically, AGI was defined first as $100B in profit generation (December 2024), then changed to an independent expert panel (October 2025), rendering the clause increasingly unenforceable. Microsoft remains OpenAI's largest shareholder. The restructuring gives OpenAI strategic freedom to diversify its cloud infrastructure while Microsoft retains a commercial relationship through 2030.

## 2. GPT 5.5 Completes Six-Hour Autonomous Task and Cracks Unsolved Hardware Reverse-Engineering

GPT 5.5 has demonstrated a new threshold of autonomous agent capability in extended-task execution, according to hands-on reviews published this week. In one documented case, a user issued a single "I trust you, figure it out" prompt and GPT 5.5 autonomously executed a 2-million-row data migration over approximately six hours with zero follow-up prompts and only one approval request. Separately, GPT 5.5 successfully reverse-engineered a proprietary Bluetooth bitmap encoding and transport mechanism that no other AI model could crack — a milestone in autonomous hardware analysis. GPT 5.5 Pro is priced at $30 per million input tokens and $180 per million output tokens, but reviewers note it remains cheaper than equivalent human engineering time for complex tasks. In the same review cycle, Claude Design was found to excel at marketing asset generation while struggling with product UX tasks, and GPT Images 2.0 now generates multi-page brand kits with proper text rendering and typography.

## 3. Anthropic's Automated Alignment Researchers Achieve 0.97 PGR, Nearly Closing the Performance Gap at $18K

Anthropic's Automated Alignment Researchers (AARs) — autonomous Claude Opus 4.6 agents running for 800 hours on weak-to-strong supervision research — have achieved a Performance Gap Recovery (PGR) score of 0.97 versus a human expert baseline of 0.23, according to Import AI. The experiment cost approximately $18,000, roughly $22 per AAR hour. The research identified designing evaluations as the key bottleneck in automated alignment work, not generating hypotheses. In a complementary safety evaluation, Kimi K2.5 was found to have similar dual-use capabilities to GPT-5.2 and Claude Opus 4.5 but with fewer refusals on CBRNE-related tasks; researchers demonstrated that with under $500 of compute and 10 hours of red-teaming, HarmBench refusal rates could be reduced from 100% to 5%, illustrating how easily safety refusals can be circumvented. Huawei also released HiFloat4, a 4-bit precision format for training and inference on Ascend NPUs that achieves ~1.0% relative loss versus ~1.5% for MXFP4.

## 4. China Orders Meta to Unwind $2B Acquisition of AI Agent Startup Manus

China's National Development and Reform Commission (NDRC) has ordered Meta to unwind its approximately $2 billion acquisition of Manus, a Singapore-based AI agent startup with Chinese origins. The order comes after a review launched by China's Ministry of Commerce in January 2026. Manus had relocated its headquarters from mainland China to Singapore in mid-2025 before the acquisition was announced, widely interpreted as an attempt to avoid regulatory scrutiny from both Beijing and Washington. Approximately 100 Manus employees had already moved into Meta's Singapore offices as of March 2026. Manus founders are reportedly under exit bans preventing them from leaving mainland China. Manus had raised $75 million from US VC Benchmark and claimed $100 million ARR within eight months of launch — the fastest startup in history to reach that milestone. US lawmakers had previously prohibited American investors from backing Chinese AI companies. The acquisition prohibition is among the most significant Chinese regulatory interventions in a cross-border AI deal to date, signaling heightened scrutiny of AI-related transactions under China's foreign investment security review mechanism.

## 5. GitHub Copilot Switches from Request-Count to Token-Based Billing on June 1

GitHub Copilot is retiring its premium request unit (PRU) model in favor of GitHub AI Credits based on actual token consumption, effective June 1, 2026. Base plan pricing remains unchanged — Pro stays at $10/month, Pro+ at $39/month, Business at $19/user/month, and Enterprise at $39/user/month. Code completions and Next Edit suggestions remain free. A preview billing tool launches in early May so users can estimate costs before the change takes effect; Copilot Business customers receive three months of promotional credits. Copilot code review also begins consuming GitHub Actions minutes. The shift was driven by agentic coding workflows that generate high token volumes under the old per-request model, making the PRU structure economically unsustainable. This is Copilot's first pricing overhaul since its launch, and it aligns billing more closely with actual compute cost — a significant change for teams running long autonomous coding sessions.

## 6. Open-Source Dirac Coding Agent Tops TerminalBench at 65.2%, 64.8% Cheaper Than Rivals

Dirac, an open-source coding agent, has topped the TerminalBench 2.0 leaderboard with a score of 65.2% using Gemini-3-flash-preview, outperforming Google's official benchmark baseline of 47.6% and the previously top-ranked closed-source agent Junie CLI at 64.3%. Dirac achieved this while reducing API costs by 64.8% versus the competition. The agent uses hash-anchored parallel edits, AST manipulation, multi-file batching, and optimized context curation to maximize efficiency — demonstrating that context optimization matters more than raw model scale for coding agent performance. Dirac is a fork of Cline and is Apache 2.0 licensed. The result underscores the rapid convergence between open and closed-source agents on rigorous terminal benchmarks, with cost efficiency becoming a primary differentiator.

## 7. DeepSeek V4 Flash API Cache Price Drops to 0.02 Yuan per Million Tokens

DeepSeek has cut the cache-hit price for its V4 series API by 90%, effective April 26, 2026, making the DeepSeek-V4-Flash model available at just 0.02 yuan (~$0.003) per million input tokens when the cache hits. The DeepSeek-V4-Pro model costs 0.025 yuan per million tokens under the same conditions, and currently carries an additional 2.5折 (60% off) limited-time discount through May 5, 2026. DeepSeek-V4-Flash offers 284B total parameters with 13B active in MoE configuration, 160GB on disk, and a 1 million token context window — using only 10% of the FLOPs and 7% of the KV cache versus the V3.2 model at 1M-token context. DeepSeek-V4-Pro has 1.6T total parameters with 49B active and is MIT licensed, likely making it the largest open-weights model available. The aggressive pricing positions DeepSeek-V4-Flash as one of the cheapest LLM APIs globally at this capability tier.
